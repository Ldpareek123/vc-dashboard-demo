EPF Wage Basis — Company Policy

This is SSPI's own policy choice, not a fixed rule. Pick whichever basis matches how SSPI is actually registered/contributing today. The default (₹15,000 ceiling) is the statutory minimum and the safer default if you are unsure.

EPS (Pension Scheme) contribution and EDLI are always capped at a ₹15,000 wage basis by law, regardless of which option you pick here — only the EPF (Provident Fund) portion and the employee's 12% deduction change with this setting.
Rates below are current as of June 2026, verified against ESIC and EPFO sources. These rates have not changed since the last government revision (ESI: July 2019; EPF: long-standing). Always confirm with your compliance consultant before filing — this bot is a calculation aid, not a filing authority.

ESI (Employees' State Insurance)

ItemRate / Limit
Employee contribution0.75% of gross monthly wages
Employer contribution3.25% of gross monthly wages
Wage ceiling for coverage₹21,000/month gross (₹25,000 for employees with disability)
Employee-share exemptionAverage daily wage ≤ ₹176 — employee share waived, employer still pays 3.25%
Contribution periodsApr–Sep and Oct–Mar. Once covered at the start of a period, an employee stays covered (and contributions continue) for the rest of that period even if wages rise above the ceiling mid-period.
Payment due date15th of the following month

EPF (Employees' Provident Fund)

ItemRate / Limit
Employee contribution12% of PF wage (Basic + DA)
Employer contribution12% total — split 3.67% to EPF + 8.33% to EPS
EPS cap8.33% is always capped at ₹15,000 wage basis (max ₹1,250/month) regardless of actual basic
Admin charges (employer, establishment-level)0.50% of PF wage, minimum ₹75/month per establishment
EDLI (employer)0.50% of PF wage, capped at ₹15,000 basis
Mandatory registration threshold20+ employees
Payment due date15th of the following month
Sources: ESIC contribution rate and wage ceiling (frozen since July 2019); EPFO contribution rate circular (epfindia.gov.in). Verified via web search on the date this bot was built — re-check before relying on these figures for a future filing period, as government notifications can change them.
